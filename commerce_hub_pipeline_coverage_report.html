TL;DR for leadership

HubSpot has $6.5M in open Commerce Hub pipeline across 3,828 deals. Four RSS owners cover 112 of them. The remaining 97.1% have no commercial specialist owner. 136 Solutions Engineers are informally engaged on 1,110 deals with no RSS attached — ungoverned pre-sales at scale, with zero attribution. EMEA, LATAM, and APAC have 602 open deals and effectively zero RSS coverage. This is not a routing problem. It is a structural argument for a coverage division.

Recommendations
Five actions to close the gap
01 — highest impact
Build a coverage division for non-NAM markets
EMEA, LATAM, and APAC have 602 open deals and zero RSS coverage. At 50 deals per rep, covering 3,828 deals requires 76 RSS owners. We have 4. A Specialist SE or Enablement Division is the structural answer.
02 — formalize SE involvement
Instrument 136 informal SEs in CRM
Add a mandatory SE-involvement property on every Commerce Hub deal before close. This creates the influenced MRR dataset needed to justify a formal overlay and make the 136 SEs' work visible to finance.
03 — quick win
Attach SE to 29 remaining RSS deals
RSS deals have a 74% SE attach rate vs. 27% overall. The 29 RSS-owned deals without SE are the highest-probability quick wins — already have a commercial owner, just need SE routing. Claffey holds 18 of those 29.
04 — prioritize by value
Target Mid Market + Corporate in NA first
NA Small Business has 423 uncovered deals but lower deal sizes. NA Mid Market ($188K uncovered) and NA Corporate ($166K uncovered) are higher-value per deal. Route by value, not just volume.
05 — infra fix
Add RSS/SE fields to HubScout semantic model
REVENUE_SALES_SPECIALIST_OWNER_53 and SOLUTIONS_ENGINEER_P53_OWNER_ID are not in HubScout — self-service coverage analysis is impossible today. Contact #dwi-infra-support.
The bottom line
This is a $6.1M structural problem
At $4–12× payments multiplier per $1 CPQ license, downstream MRR at risk from uncovered pipeline far exceeds the pipeline face value. The decision is not whether to invest in coverage — it's which model and how fast.
