Section 1 Executive Summary

The period May 10 – June 9 marks a decisive inflection in the Revenue Hub GTM motion: the shift from build to launch. Three structural bets that have been in construction for twelve months — a unified Revenue Sales Specialist team, a systematic field enablement program, and an AI-native sales motion — are now in simultaneous execution.

Three events in a single week define this moment. The Revenue Hub June 16 launch received a formal GO on June 4 with all P0/P1 blockers closed. The Deal Support Request round-robin routing system went live June 1, on schedule. The SST org restructure — moving fully out of SMB into upmarket — was confirmed June 5. These are the delivery of commitments made at the start of 2026 and the activation of infrastructure that makes the Revenue Hub field motion repeatable at scale.

The commercial reality entering launch week: CPQ is tracking at $19M vs. $35M target; Payments at approximately $45M vs. $54M target. The combined $64M against an $89M goal means the post-June 16 motion — enabled SST, live DSR routing, AI-powered prospecting, reinforced partner ecosystem — is not optional acceleration. It is a required one. The launch is the starting gun, not the finish line.

Simultaneously, an org integration proposal — absorbing three Commerce Specialists into the RSS org to create a unified seven-person Revenue Hub team — represents the most significant structural bet of the period. The economic rationale ($89M Revenue Hub) and operational logic (Payments conversations invariably become CPQ conversations) are both documented. The decision is now an approval process, not a strategy debate.

Section 4 Active Projects & Strategic Initiatives
P1
Revenue Hub June 16 Launch
Target: June 16, 2026  ·  Scope: CPQ + Contracts + Billing + Payments  ·  Status: GO declared June 4

Shipping June 16: Connected CPQ → billing → payments; Contract object as source of record (ACV, TCV, ARR, MRR); automated billing schedules with proration; price books with auto-assignment rules; QuickBooks integration improvements; Revenue Agent (collections) entering private beta.

GTM positioning: "Revenue isn't a moment — it's a process. When that process lives under one roof, you grow better." Value framework: Speed (templates), Visibility (quote analyzer), Control (approval workflows).

The economic anchor: For every $1 of CPQ license revenue, HubSpot sees $4–$12 in Payments. Partners drive 125% MRR lift on Revenue Hub deals when attached. These numbers are the foundation of the field narrative and the investor case simultaneously.
P2
SST Enablement Program — Track 1 → Track 2 → Unbound
North Star: 100% SST at L2 before Unbound (August 11+)  ·  Status: Week 4 of 5 in Track 1

Track 1 (10 hand-picked specialists, May 19 – June 16) is the proof-of-concept for the certification program design. Track 2 (full SST, June 23 – July 25) scales what T1 validates. The Unbound launch in August is the market moment that activates the full SST Revenue Hub motion. Eight enablement sessions recorded in this period including guest speakers Christian Sibley (Customer Agent + Revenue) and Joe Warren (QBO Connector).

Certification architecture: L1 (Harbor Tour — 5-min value walkthrough) → L2 (End-to-End Seller — 30-min full demo with live objections) → L3 (Prescriptive Build — 60-min live deal brief). Tenor AI handles post-cert async reinforcement monthly.

P3
DSR Round Robin — Deal Support Request Routing
Status: LIVE — June 1, 2026 ✅  ·  Routing: <10 licenses → partner; 10–50 → rotation; 50+ → senior escalation

Converts the informal "ping in Slack" model into a trackable, attributable, CRM-logged workflow across ~2,700 GS reps. Routing is directly tied to specialist certification status — certified reps receive deals, uncertified do not. Initial parameters: no license floor restrictions; reassess 2 weeks post-launch. Ed/Cam/Pak handle the 10–50 license rotation; 50+ escalates.

P4
SST AI GTM — Claude Cowork Activation & SST Workspace
Onsite: May 19–20 (10 attendees)  ·  SST Workspace launched: May 27  ·  MVP target: June 1–8

The May 19–20 AI onsite produced a unified Claude-powered sales plugin across three workflows: Prospecting (minor tweaks needed), Discovery (ready), Demo (pre-demo email + bridge testing in progress). Claude Cowork is now the standard AI tool across all SST — replacing ChatGPT and Gemini. The SST Workspace auto-populates the customer book with six signal types: competitor renewals, credit limits, Customer Agent signals, predicted upsell/cross-sell, usage-based triggers, and Revenue Hub intent keywords.

10x experiment: One SST team is incentivized to hit 2x targets over 3 months using the full AI tool stack — the most direct test of whether AI tooling translates to measurable sales productivity.

CRM Deal Attribution — Building the Habit of Accurate Activity Logging
Applies to: Full team · Process established June 1
In Progress
The DSR system went live June 1. For deal credit to flow correctly, two activity types must be logged consistently: "Connected" outcome and "External Demo" activity type. This is a new process, and as with any new system, adoption takes time to establish as a habit. The good news: the system design is sound, the routing is working, and the attribution logic is in CRM. What remains is behavioral reinforcement — building the logging habit across the team within the first 60 days of DSR being live.
The Plan
June 16: Attribution audit at 2-week mark post-DSR launch June 30: Target — 100% activity logging compliance across team July standup: Weekly CRM hygiene review added to RSS Stand-up agenda

Measurement: % of DSR-routed deals with "Connected" outcome logged; % of customer meetings logged as "External Demo" activity type. Tracked weekly in the Revenue Round Up.

Partner Co-Sell Discipline — Closing the 40-Point Attach Gap
Applies to: Full team · Current: ~50% attach · Target: 90%
In Progress
The data makes the case plainly: partner-attached deals close at 97% higher ASP ($1,093 vs $554). The gap between 50% and 90% attach is not a partner availability problem — the VIP Partner Kick-Off happened May 28, the DSR routing explicitly directs sub-10-license deals to partner-first, and the "Quote-to-Cash Accelerator" badge is launching. This is a team habit and deal-level discipline issue. On every DSR submission, the question "is there a partner on this?" needs to become as automatic as the deal amount field. The infrastructure exists. The behavior just needs to catch up.
The Plan
Immediate: Partner name required field on DSR form July: Partner attach rate reviewed in every Revenue Round Up August 11+: Unbound Launch — partner co-sell is the primary go-to-market motion

Measurement: Partner attach rate by rep (monthly); ASP by deal with/without partner (quarterly). Target: 70% attach rate by August 1, 90% by Unbound.
